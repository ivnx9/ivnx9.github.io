=== TOPIC: hydroponics_overview
TAGS: hydroponics, basics, overview, whatis, paano, explanation
TEXT:
Hydroponics is growing plants using nutrient-rich water instead of soil. Key control points are pH, nutrient strength (TDS/EC), temperature, oxygenation, and consistent light. Small changes can cause nutrient lockout or slow growth.
=== END

=== TOPIC: ph_definition
TAGS: ph, acidity, alkaline, whatisph, anoangph
TEXT:
pH measures how acidic or alkaline the solution is (0–14). 7 is neutral. Below 7 is acidic, above 7 is alkaline. In hydroponics, pH affects nutrient availability—wrong pH can cause nutrient lockout even if TDS is “correct.”
=== END

=== TOPIC: ec_tds_definition
TAGS: tds, ec, ppm, nutrients, whatistds, whatisec
TEXT:
TDS (ppm) estimates dissolved solids, often used as a proxy for nutrient strength. EC is electrical conductivity and is a more direct measurement. Higher TDS/EC usually means stronger nutrient solution. Too high can burn roots; too low can cause slow growth or deficiency.
=== END

=== TOPIC: lettuce_ideal_ph
TAGS: lettuce, letsugas, ph, ideal, range, recommended
TEXT:
Lettuce commonly grows well around pH 5.8–6.5. If pH is too high, iron/manganese and other micronutrients may lock out. If too low, roots can stress and uptake patterns change. Adjust gradually.
=== END

=== TOPIC: pechay_ideal_ph
TAGS: pechay, bokchoy, mustardgreens, ph, ideal
TEXT:
Pechay/bok choy generally does well around pH 5.8–6.5. Keep changes gradual. Sudden pH swings stress roots and can cause wilting or stunted growth.
=== END

=== TOPIC: lettuce_ideal_tds
TAGS: lettuce, tds, ppm, ideal, range, seedling, veg, mature
TEXT:
Typical lettuce nutrient strength ranges depend on stage:
- Seedlings: ~300–600 ppm
- Vegetative: ~600–900 ppm
- Mature/fast growth: ~800–1200 ppm
If tips burn or edges brown, reduce ppm and check heat/light stress.
=== END

=== TOPIC: pechay_ideal_tds
TAGS: pechay, bokchoy, tds, ppm, ideal, range
TEXT:
Pechay often tolerates slightly higher strength than lettuce, but stage matters:
- Seedlings: ~400–700 ppm
- Vegetative: ~700–1100 ppm
- Mature: ~900–1400 ppm
If leaves curl or burn, lower ppm and check temperature.
=== END

=== TOPIC: water_temp_basics
TAGS: water, temperature, water_temp, solution_temp, roots
TEXT:
Water temperature affects oxygen levels and root health. Warmer water holds less dissolved oxygen and can invite root issues. Many leafy greens do well when solution temperature is roughly in the high teens to low 20s °C, but stability matters most.
=== END

=== TOPIC: air_temp_basics
TAGS: airtemp, temperature, environment, heat, cold
TEXT:
Air temperature affects transpiration and growth rate. Too hot = faster water loss + stress; too cold = slow growth. Pair temp control with humidity control to avoid extreme VPD stress.
=== END

=== TOPIC: humidity_basics
TAGS: humidity, rh, relativehumidity, damp, tuyo
TEXT:
Humidity affects how fast plants transpire. Low humidity can cause fast water loss; high humidity can reduce transpiration and increase disease risk. Aim for stable RH and good airflow.
=== END

=== TOPIC: ph_high_symptoms
TAGS: ph, highph, symptoms, yellowing, chlorosis, lockout
TEXT:
If pH is too high, you may see yellowing between leaf veins (chlorosis), slow growth, or signs of micronutrient deficiency even though nutrients are present. Confirm using a calibrated pH sensor or test kit before correcting.
=== END

=== TOPIC: ph_low_symptoms
TAGS: ph, lowph, symptoms, stress, roots, stunted
TEXT:
If pH is too low, roots can be stressed and plants can show overall poor uptake. You might notice slow growth, droop, or odd deficiencies. Correct gradually to avoid shock.
=== END

=== TOPIC: tds_too_high_symptoms
TAGS: tds, ppm, too_high, burn, tipsburn, leafburn
TEXT:
If TDS/ppm is too high, common signs include leaf tip burn, edge burn, curled leaves, or a “crispy” look. Lower ppm gradually, and check if high temperature/light is amplifying stress.
=== END

=== TOPIC: tds_too_low_symptoms
TAGS: tds, ppm, too_low, deficiency, pale, slowgrowth
TEXT:
If TDS/ppm is too low, plants may look pale, grow slowly, or show nutrient deficiency patterns. Increase nutrient strength slowly and verify pH is in range first.
=== END

=== TOPIC: ph_adjustment_rules
TAGS: ph, adjust, paano, step, procedure, rule
TEXT:
Safe pH adjustment rules:
1) Measure and confirm reading (calibration matters).
2) Adjust in small steps.
3) Mix well and wait before re-measuring.
4) Avoid large daily swings.
5) Track what you added so you can learn your system’s behavior.
=== END

=== TOPIC: tds_adjustment_rules
TAGS: tds, ppm, adjust, dilute, addnutrients, steps
TEXT:
Safe TDS adjustment rules:
- To lower ppm: add clean water (dilution), mix, re-measure.
- To raise ppm: add nutrients in small amounts, mix, re-measure.
- Don’t chase numbers too aggressively—plant response + stability matters.
=== END

=== TOPIC: sensor_calibration_ph_general
TAGS: ph, calibration, calibrate, buffer, 4.0, 7.0, 10.0
TEXT:
pH calibration is crucial. If using a pH probe, calibrate with known buffer solutions (commonly pH 4.0 and 7.0; sometimes 10.0). Rinse probe between solutions. A poorly calibrated sensor will make your corrections worse.
=== END

=== TOPIC: sensor_calibration_tds_general
TAGS: tds, ec, calibration, calibrate, solution
TEXT:
TDS/EC sensor calibration uses a known conductivity calibration solution. Temperature affects EC, so temperature compensation (automatic or manual) matters. If your readings drift a lot, recalibrate and check wiring/noise.
=== END

=== TOPIC: pump_control_best_practice
TAGS: pump, waterpump, circulation, schedule, timer, onoff
TEXT:
For pumps: prioritize consistency, avoid frequent rapid switching that can reduce lifespan, and ensure water circulation is enough to prevent stagnation. If using relays, consider minimum on/off times to protect hardware.
=== END

=== TOPIC: growlight_basics
TAGS: growlight, light, ppfd, photoperiod, schedule
TEXT:
Light drives growth. Too little light = slow growth and stretching. Too much light (especially with heat) = stress and tip burn. A consistent photoperiod (daily light schedule) is usually better than random on/off times.
=== END

=== TOPIC: algae_prevention
TAGS: algae, greenwater, prevention, cover, lightleak
TEXT:
Algae grows when light reaches nutrient water. Prevention: cover reservoirs, block light leaks, use opaque tubing, and keep surfaces clean. Algae competes for nutrients and can mess up sensors.
=== END

=== TOPIC: root_health_signs
TAGS: roots, roothealth, brown, slime, smell
TEXT:
Healthy roots are usually light-colored and not slimy. Warning signs: brown roots, slime, foul smell, and stunted plant growth. Improve oxygenation, water temperature stability, cleanliness, and avoid overfeeding.
=== END

=== TOPIC: troubleshooting_wilting
TAGS: wilting, droop, halaman, tulala, troubleshoot
TEXT:
Wilting can come from heat stress, root problems, low oxygen, severe pH imbalance, or sudden TDS changes. Check: water temp, pump circulation, pH calibration, ppm strength, and airflow. Fix one thing at a time.
=== END

=== TOPIC: troubleshooting_tipburn
TAGS: tipburn, leafburn, lettuce, edges, brown
TEXT:
Tip burn often relates to fast growth + calcium transport issues, commonly worsened by heat, low airflow, or too strong nutrients. Improve airflow, reduce heat, ensure stable watering/circulation, and avoid excessive ppm.
=== END
